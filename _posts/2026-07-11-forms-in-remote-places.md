excerpt: "A few thoughts from a walk: stone, moss, paths, and the strange patience of things that grow without an audience."

The mountains do not hurry. Stone holds its shape long enough for moss to make a different kind of time visible.

Walking is one way of reading a place. You notice not only what is there, but what has been allowed to persist.
