title: A note on keeping a commonplace book
category: Field note
excerpt: "A notebook is less a record of certainty than a way to give unfinished thoughts somewhere to live."
---

There are ideas that do not ask to be solved. They ask to be kept near enough to change us.

A commonplace book gives those ideas a shelf. It gathers fragments without forcing them into a conclusion: a passage from a book, a question overheard in conversation, a sentence that stays with you long after its context has gone.

The point is not to remember everything. It is to make a small climate in which thought can return.
